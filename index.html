About

Pioneering from Film to Design:
Crafting Stories Through Purposeful Creation

A little on my past

Up until now I have been a self-taught filmmaker (cinematographer, producer, director, editor) and have worked tirelessly to develop an expertise from a fundamental belief that great stories have the power to heal, liberate, educate, and entertain. Throughout my filmmaking career, I aimed to illustrate that through film we can discover a connection to something more profound than our own perspective, and as such my work showcases the lifeblood of our shared human condition.

At the present

So, why move to Product Design? Hear me out...
After a long stint in filmmaking, I'm seeking fresh creative challenges. Driven by my fascination with human behavior and psychology, I'm drawn to the user-centered, purposeful ethos of product design. Completing a UX/UI bootcamp at Monash University, I see the potential for my filmmaking skills to shine in this field. Storytelling, interviewing, visual communication, and narrative craft—all honed in filmmaking—blend seamlessly with design thinking, enriching my process to craft more human-centered, purpose-driven experiences.

To get to the future

My career goals involve a continuous journey of growth and development as a product designer, with the ultimate aim of becoming a Creative Director. This path will require a combination of skill-building, leadership development, strategic thinking, and a commitment to creative excellence.
